Comparatives in English

What is a comparative?

Comparatives in English are used to compare two things, people, or places. They are usually formed by adding -er to the adjective or using more before the adjective.

What are they used for?

They are used to show differences, for example:

This book is shorter than the other one.

General rules:

  • Short adjectives (1 syllable): add -er → tall → taller
  • Adjectives ending in "e": just add -r → large → larger
  • Consonant-vowel-consonant adjectives: double the last letter → big → bigger
  • Long adjectives (2 or more syllables): use more → beautiful → more beautiful
